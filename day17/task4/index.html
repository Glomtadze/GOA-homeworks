Albert Einstein

Albert Einstein was one of the most influential scientists in history, best known for revolutionizing our understanding of space, time, and energy. Born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, Germany, Einstein showed an early curiosity about how the world works, though he was not always considered a top student in traditional schooling. His deep interest in mathematics and physics eventually led him to pursue a career in theoretical science.

Einstein is most famous for developing the Theory of Relativity, which transformed the way scientists understand gravity, motion, and the structure of the universe. His equation, E = mc², revealed the relationship between mass and energy, demonstrating that even a small amount of matter can be converted into a tremendous amount of energy. This discovery later played a crucial role in the development of nuclear power and atomic weapons.


biography

Albert Einstein was born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, in the Kingdom of Württemberg, part of the German Empire. He grew up in a middle-class Jewish family; his father, Hermann Einstein, was an engineer and businessman, while his mother, Pauline Koch, encouraged his interest in music and learning. As a child, Einstein was quiet and thoughtful, and although he did not excel in every subject at school, he showed a strong ability in mathematics and physics from an early age.

Einstein’s education took place in Germany, Italy, and later Switzerland. He attended the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich, where he trained as a teacher in physics and mathematics. After graduating in 1900, he struggled to find a teaching position and instead worked at a patent office in Bern. During this time, he continued his scientific research independently, developing ideas that would later change the world.
