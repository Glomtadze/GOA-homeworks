Shutter Island is a psychological thriller released in 2010. It was directed by Martin Scorsese. The main character, Teddy Daniels, is played by Leonardo DiCaprio. He and his partner arrive at Ashecliffe Hospital on a remote island. They are investigating the mysterious disappearance of a patient. The hospital holds mentally ill criminals and is surrounded by strict security. As Teddy continues the investigation, he starts to question everything.

He experiences flashbacks related to World War II and his personal life. The weather on the island becomes stormy, adding tension to the story. Doctors and staff begin to act strangely, making Teddy more suspicious. The film builds a sense of mystery and psychological tension. In the end, a dramatic twist reveals the truth about Teddy’s identity and reality. The movie explores deep themes like guilt, trauma, and perception of truth. Shutter Island is known for its complex story and powerful performances.
