Muhammad ali

I was born **Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr.** on January 17, 1942, in Louisville. I grew up in a segregated America, where opportunities for Black people were limited. When I was 12 years old, someone stole my bicycle. Upset and angry, I told a police officer I wanted to “whup” whoever did it. That officer, Joe Martin, introduced me to boxing — and that changed my life forever. I trained hard and quickly discovered I had speed, confidence, and a style no one had seen before. In 1960, I won a gold medal at the 1960 Summer Olympics in Rome. Soon after, I turned professional and began climbing the ranks of heavyweight boxing. In 1964, I shocked the world by defeating Sonny Liston to become the heavyweight champion of the world. Shortly after that victory, I announced I had joined the Nation of Islam and changed my name to Muhammad Ali. I said, “Cassius Clay is my slave name.”

Throughout the 1960s, I defended my title and became known not just for my boxing skills, but for my bold personality and poetic trash talk. I would say things like, “Float like a butterfly, sting like a bee.” In 1967, I refused to be drafted into the Vietnam War because of my religious beliefs and opposition to the war. As a result, I was stripped of my boxing title and banned from the sport for several years. It was one of the toughest periods of my life, but I stood by my principles. I returned to boxing in the 1970s and fought some of the greatest matches in history, including the “Fight of the Century” against Joe Frazier and the legendary “Rumble in the Jungle” against George Foreman in Kinshasa, where I regained the heavyweight title. Over my career, I became a three-time heavyweight champion and one of the most recognized athletes in the world. Later in life, I was diagnosed with Parkinsons disease, but I continued to travel, do charity work, and inspire millions with messages of peace and faith. I passed away on June 3, 2016, but my legacy lives on — not just as a boxer, but as a man who stood up for his beliefs and inspired people around the world. “I am the greatest.”
