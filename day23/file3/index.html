Tennis

Tennis is a popular sport played between two players (singles) or two teams of two players (doubles). It is played on a rectangular court divided by a net. The main objective of the game is to hit the ball over the net into the opponent's court in such a way that they cannot return it successfully. Tennis can be played on different surfaces, including grass, clay, and hard courts. Each surface affects the speed and bounce of the ball, making the game more dynamic and strategic.

Players use a racket to hit a felt-covered rubber ball. Points are scored when the opponent fails to return the ball within the court boundaries. A match is typically divided into sets, and each set consists of games. The scoring system in tennis is unique, using terms like “love” (zero), “15,” “30,” and “40.” Tennis requires a combination of physical fitness, agility, coordination, and mental focus. It is played professionally worldwide, with major tournaments such as Wimbledon, the US Open, the French Open, and the Australian Open. Overall, tennis is both a competitive and recreational sport enjoyed by millions of people around the world.


Roger Federer

Roger Federer is one of the greatest tennis players in history, known for his smooth playing style, precision,and consistency. He was born on August 8, 1981, in Basel, Switzerland. Federer turned professional in 1998 and quickly rose to the top of the sport. Over his career, he won 20 Grand Slam singles titles, including a record eight titles at Wimbledon, which is played on grass courts. Federer was ranked world No. 1 for a total of 310 weeks, including a record 237 consecutive weeks. His main rivals during his career included Rafael Nadal and Novak Djokovic, and their matches are considered some of the greatest in tennis history.

He is admired not only for his success but also for his sportsmanship and calm behavior on the court. Federer officially retired from professional tennis in 2022 after playing his final match at the Laver Cup. Off the court, he is also known for his charity work through the Roger Federer Foundation, which supports education projects for children in Africa and Switzerland.
